Main Features

Login and user maintenance

-Login page with 'Forgot Password' link

-Forgot Password form emails user a token for resetting their password that expires in 24 hours

-Login form delays 3 seconds after every failed login attempt and displays a captcha after 3 failed attempts

-User maintenance is handled through a single form for both authentication and profile fields

-Standard values defined for access groups and assignments with the ability to add custom values for each

Overtime Tracking

-Overtime entry form utilizes drop down with all employees and loads the form using AJAX

-Review overtime form displays a single overtime record per page with next, previous, first and last navigation buttons. Also is view only by default and allows shift leaders access to edit overtime records

-Overtime report displays the mandatory recall list per shift sorted by the most time elapsed since working an overtime shift first. The report will sort by overtime day and shift worked and will look at the 3 most recent overtime shifts worked. Also includes a link to a PDF version of the report formatted for printing.

Timeline history of EMS

  • Biblical times: The story of the good Samaritan
  • 1732: Mouth to mouth ventilation
  • 1861: EMS in America can be traced back to the Civil War
  • 1865: First civilian ambulance in Cincinnati
  • 1869: NYC advertises a 30 second response time and provides an ambulance surgeon and a quart of brandy for their patients
  • 1878: Treatment established on how to restore breathing and circulation
  • 1950: Modern EMS began in the 1950's. There were 5 different types of ambulance services: Towing operators, medical equipment companies, funeral homes, hospital and police/fire departments.
  • 1979: The Journal of Emergency Medical Services (JEMS) starts publication
  • 1985: The National Association of EMS Physicians is established
  • 1990: The Trauma Care System Planning and Development Act is passed by congress
